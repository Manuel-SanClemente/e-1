Mammoth

Also known as a "mastodon"

A mammoth is a member of the genus Mammut, which, strictly defined, was endemic to North America and lived from the late Miocene to the early Holocene.

Facts

Here are some facts about them:

  • They were about the size of a modern African elephant.
  • Mammoths used their curved tusks to dig up the ground.
  • We can tell the age of a woolly mammoth from the rings of its tusk
